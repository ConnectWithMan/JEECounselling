Overview: JEE Main 2027 Reservation Policy

The Government of India enforces a structured reservation policy for admissions into Centrally Funded Technical Institutions (CFTIs) like NITs, IIITs, and GFTIs. This policy designates specific percentages of seats for socially and economically backward sections, including Other Backward Classes (OBC-NCL), Scheduled Castes (SC), Scheduled Tribes (ST), and General-Economically Weaker Sections (Gen-EWS). Detailed guidelines on who qualifies are also linked in the JEE Main Eligibility Criteria.

In addition to these vertical categories, a horizontal reservation of 5% is carved out for Persons with Disabilities (PwD) within each group. Domicile-based reservations, known as the Home State Quota, also apply to NITs, reserving 50% of their total intake for regional candidates during JoSAA Counselling.

Key Concept: Vertical reservations allocate a fixed percentage of seats, while horizontal reservations (like PwD) are applied within each vertical pool. If a candidate qualifies on pure open merit, they are allotted an Open seat, preserving the reserved seat for other candidates.

Category-Wise Seat Reservation Percentages

Below is the official reservation percentage structure followed by JoSAA / CSAB for JEE Main qualified candidates:

General-EWS Reservation Criteria

Introduced in 2019, the Economically Weaker Section (EWS) reservation reserves 10% of seats for General category candidates who do not belong to SC, ST, or OBC-NCL, and whose family income falls below the prescribed thresholds.

Income and Asset Requirements:

  • Annual Family Income: Must be less than ₹8 Lakh from all sources (gross annual income) during the preceding financial year.
  • Agricultural Land: Family must not own 5 acres or more of agricultural land.
  • Residential Flat: Must not own a residential flat of 1000 sq. ft. or more.
  • Residential Plot (Urban): Must not own a plot of 100 sq. yards or more in notified municipalities.
  • Residential Plot (Rural): Must not own a plot of 200 sq. yards or more in non-notified municipalities.

EWS Certificate Validity: The EWS certificate must be issued by a Tehsildar or above in the prescribed Government of India format after April 1, 2027. State-format income certificates are not accepted as proof.

OBC-NCL Reservation Guidelines

Candidates belonging to the Other Backward Classes (Non-Creamy Layer) are eligible for a 27% seat reservation at NITs, IIITs, and GFTIs during the admission process. Make sure to specify this during your JEE Main Application Form registration.

Central List Rule: To claim OBC-NCL reservation, the candidate's caste must be included in the Central List of OBCs published by the Ministry of Social Justice and Empowerment (available at socialjustice.gov.in). Castes listed in state OBC lists but absent from the Central List are considered General category in JEE Main.

Parameters Details
Gross Family Income Limit Must be less than ₹8 Lakh per annum (excluding agricultural income) for the consecutive 3 financial years.

SC / ST Category Quotas & Relaxations

Scheduled Caste (SC) candidates receive 15% reservation, and Scheduled Tribe (ST) candidates receive 7.5% reservation across all CFTIs.

Relaxations Offered:

  • Class 12 Board Percentage: The qualifying threshold for NIT/IIIT/GFTI admission is relaxed to 65% aggregate (compared to 75% for General/OBC-NCL/EWS) as detailed in the JEE Main Eligibility Criteria.
  • Percentile Cutoff: A significantly lower qualifying rank is applied for qualifying to JEE Advanced (refer to the JEE Main Cutoff page for details).
  • Application Fee: SC/ST/PwD candidates pay reduced registration fees.
  • No Expiry Date: SC/ST certificates do not require yearly renewal, but they must be in the prescribed Central Government format and state the specific tribe/caste matching the presidential order.

PwD Horizontal Reservation

Candidates with physical disabilities are eligible for a 5% horizontal reservation within each category. This means seats are allocated as General-PwD, OBC-NCL-PwD, SC-PwD, ST-PwD, and EWS-PwD pools. Look at the JoSAA Seat Matrix for individual college seat numbers.

  • Disability Benchmark: Minimum of 40% benchmark disability is mandatory, certified by an authorized Government Medical Board. Private doctor certificates are invalid.
  • Exam Scribe Facility: PwD candidates can opt for a scribe during the JEE Main CBT and receive 20 minutes of compensatory time per hour.
  • Physical Verification: During JoSAA Counselling, PwD candidates must report to a designated reporting center for physical verification by a medical board to confirm their disability status.

NIT Home State (HS) vs Other State (OS) Quotas

National Institutes of Technology (NITs) distribute their seats in a 50:50 ratio under two categories. You can see past cutoffs for home states on our JEE Main Cutoff page.

Home State Quota (50%)

Reserved for candidates who completed and passed their Class 12 board examination from a school located in the same state as the NIT. Domicile state or birth state does not impact this eligibility.

Other State Quota (50%)

Allocated to candidates applying from any other state or union territory in India. Usually requires significantly higher cutoff percentiles (AIR rank) compared to HS seats.

State of Eligibility Clarification: Your "State of Eligibility" is solely determined by the location of the school where you wrote the Class 12 exam. For example, if you live in Bihar but completed Class 12 in Jharkhand, your Home State NIT is NIT Jamshedpur (Jharkhand), not NIT Patna (Bihar).

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

OBC-NCL candidates get 27% reservation in JEE Main seat allotment at NITs, IIITs, and GFTIs. At IITs, the same 27% OBC-NCL reservation applies via JEE Advanced. This is the largest reserved category in JEE Main counselling.
There is no separate OBC-NCL percentile cutoff for appearing in JEE Main. However, for JEE Advanced eligibility, a separate (lower) percentile cutoff is fixed for OBC-NCL candidates each year. For NIT/IIIT/GFTI admissions, any valid JEE Main 2027 score qualifies you for JoSAA counselling, and seats are allotted based on rank and choice filling within the OBC-NCL pool.
15% of seats are reserved for Scheduled Caste (SC) candidates in all Centrally Funded Technical Institutes, including IITs, NITs, IIITs, and GFTIs. Within these 15% SC seats, an additional 5% horizontal reservation is applied for PwD-SC candidates.
Yes, EWS reservation is exclusively for General category candidates who do not belong to SC, ST, or OBC-NCL categories. If your family's annual income is less than ₹8 lakh and you meet all other EWS property criteria, you can claim 10% EWS reservation in NITs, IIITs, and GFTIs by submitting a valid EWS certificate (issued after April 1, 2027) during JoSAA counselling.
The Home State Quota reserves 50% of NIT seats for candidates who passed Class 12 from the state in which that NIT is located. This significantly lowers the rank required to secure a seat. For example, an OBC-NCL candidate from Rajasthan may get NIT Jaipur CSE at a rank 800–1,500 higher (numerically) than what an OBC-NCL candidate from another state would need. Entering the correct State of Eligibility during JoSAA registration is critical to access this benefit.
