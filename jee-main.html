What is JEE Main 2027?

JEE Main (Joint Entrance Examination — Main) is a national-level computer-based test conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA) for admission to undergraduate engineering programs (B.E./B.Tech), architecture (B.Arch), and planning (B.Planning) at NITs, IIITs, GFTIs and other centrally funded technical institutions across India. It also serves as the qualifying examination for JEE Advanced, which leads to the prestigious IITs.

JEE Main 2027 will be conducted in two sessions — Session 1 in January 2027 and Session 2 in April 2027. Candidates can appear in either or both sessions; the best NTA Score across all appeared sessions is used for merit list preparation.

Eligibility Criteria

Academic Qualification

  • Must have passed 10+2 or equivalent in 2025, 2026, or 2027
  • Appearing candidates in 2027 are also eligible
  • Open School / NIOS results accepted
  • Indian nationals, OCI/PIO cardholders, and foreign nationals eligible

Required Subjects in Class 12

  • Mathematics — Compulsory
  • Any one of: Chemistry / Biology / Biotechnology / Technical Vocational

JEE Main 2027 Syllabus

JEE Main syllabus is based on the Class 11 and Class 12 NCERT curriculum. The syllabus may be officially revised by NTA before 2027 — always check the official NTA notification for the exact topics.

Physics

  • Units, Dimensions & Kinematics
  • Laws of Motion & Work Energy
  • Rotational Motion & Gravitation
  • Properties of Matter & Fluid Mechanics
  • Thermodynamics & Kinetic Theory
  • Oscillations & Waves
  • Electrostatics & Current Electricity
  • Magnetic Effects of Current
  • Electromagnetic Induction & AC
  • Electromagnetic Waves & Optics
  • Dual Nature of Matter & Radiation
  • Atoms, Nuclei & Electronic Devices
  • Semiconductor Electronics

Chemistry

  • Basic Concepts & Mole Concept
  • Atomic Structure & Chemical Bonding
  • States of Matter (Gas, Liquid, Solid)
  • Thermodynamics & Equilibrium
  • Redox Reactions & Electrochemistry
  • Chemical Kinetics & Surface Chemistry
  • Periodic Table & General Properties
  • s, p, d & f Block Elements
  • Coordination Compounds
  • Organic Chemistry — Basics & Reactions
  • Hydrocarbons & Haloalkanes
  • Alcohols, Phenols, Ethers & Aldehydes
  • Amines, Biomolecules & Polymers

Mathematics

  • Sets, Relations & Functions
  • Complex Numbers & Quadratic Equations
  • Sequences, Series & Progressions
  • Permutations, Combinations & Binomial
  • Limits, Continuity & Differentiability
  • Differentiation & Applications
  • Integration & Definite Integrals
  • Differential Equations
  • Vectors & 3D Geometry
  • Coordinate Geometry (Conics)
  • Trigonometry & Inverse Trig
  • Matrices & Determinants
  • Probability & Statistics

In 2023, NTA revised the JEE Main syllabus and removed certain topics. Always refer to the latest official NTA information bulletin for the exact 2027 syllabus.

Registration & Application Process

Step 1
Visit the Official NTA JEE Main Portal
Go to jeemain.nta.nic.in. Registration opens approximately 6–8 weeks before each session.
Step 2
Create New Account / Register
Enter your name, date of birth, mobile number, and email ID. You will receive login credentials via SMS/email.
Step 3
Fill Application Form
Fill in personal, academic, and contact details carefully. Choose your preferred exam city (3 choices), paper(s), and medium of exam.
Step 4
Upload Documents
Upload a recent passport-size photograph (JPEG, 10–200 KB), signature (JPEG, 4–30 KB), and Class 10 certificate if required.
Step 5
Pay Application Fee
Pay online via Credit/Debit card, Net Banking, or UPI. Fee is non-refundable. A separate form and fee is required for each session.

Download Confirmation Page
Save and print the confirmation page. Keep your Application Number safe — required for admit card, result, and counselling.

Admit Card

The JEE Main 2027 Admit Card will be released online on the official NTA portal approximately 7–10 days before the exam. It is not sent by post. Candidates must carry a printed copy to the exam hall along with one valid photo ID.

Details on Admit Card

  • Candidate's name & roll number
  • Exam date, time & shift
  • Exam centre name & address
  • Candidate's photograph & signature
  • Category & PwD status

Documents to Carry on Exam Day

  • Printed Admit Card (coloured/B&W)
  • Valid photo ID (Aadhaar / PAN / Passport / Voter ID)
  • PwD certificate (if applicable)
  • One passport-size photograph

Electronic devices (mobile phones, smartwatches, earphones, calculators) are strictly prohibited inside the exam hall. Rough paper is provided by the centre.

Result & NTA Score

JEE Main results are declared in the form of NTA Score (Percentile Score), not raw marks. The NTA Score represents the percentage of candidates who scored equal to or below that score in the exam session. It is calculated separately for each session using normalization across multiple shifts.

JEE Main 2027 Counselling

JEE Main qualifiers are eligible to participate in JoSAA and CSAB counselling for admission to NITs, IIITs, and GFTIs. State-level counselling is conducted separately by each state for state quota seats.

JoSAA Counselling

  • For IITs, NITs, IIITs, GFTIs
  • Online choice-filling and locking
  • 5–6 rounds of seat allotment
  • Document verification at reporting centre
  • Expected June–July 2027

CSAB Counselling

  • Fills vacant seats after JoSAA rounds
  • NEUT & SFWE rounds for special categories
  • Usually 2 rounds (CSAB Round 1 & 2)
  • Expected July–August 2027

JEE Main 2027 Preparation Strategy

Master NCERT First

NCERT books for Class 11 & 12 form the foundation. Complete Chemistry NCERT thoroughly — many questions are directly asked. For Physics and Maths, understand concepts deeply before moving to advanced texts.

Structured Study Plan

Create a timetable covering all 3 subjects daily. Dedicate at least 6–8 hours of focused study. Allocate more time to weakest subjects without neglecting strengths. Revise weekly.

Solve Previous Year Papers

Solve the last 10 years of JEE Main question papers. Understand the type of questions, difficulty distribution, and recurring topics. Focus on high-weightage areas.

Timed Mock Tests

Attempt full-length mock tests in exam-like conditions. Practice time management — allocate ~72 minutes per subject. Review every mistake in detail and learn from errors.

Make Formula Sheets

Maintain concise formula sheets and short notes for quick revision during the final weeks. For Chemistry, prepare topic-wise short notes on reactions and equations.

Focus on High-Weightage Topics

Physics: Mechanics, Electrostatics, Modern Physics. Chemistry: Organic Chemistry, Chemical Bonding, Equilibrium. Maths: Calculus, Coordinate Geometry, Algebra. These areas consistently carry the most marks.

Master Numerical Questions

Section B has no negative marking — never leave these blank after preparation. Practice numericals from DC Pandey, RD Sharma, and JD Lee extensively.

Take Care of Health

Sleep 7–8 hours daily, eat well, and take short breaks every 1.5–2 hours. Mental well-being is crucial for long-term preparation. Meditate or exercise to stay focused and reduce anxiety.

Recommended Books: Physics — HC Verma, DC Pandey | Chemistry — NCERT + OP Tandon (Organic), JD Lee (Inorganic) | Mathematics — RD Sharma, Cengage Series, Arihant Books.
